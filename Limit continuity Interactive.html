Question 7 Sections A & C Evaluate

A classmate argues: “If you just zoom in far enough, the two probes always end up at the same height — so every limit exists.” Use Section A and Section C to critique this claim in 3–4 sentences. Say what the classmate is confusing, and what you actually observe on each section as the magnification rises.

Model answer The claim is false. On Section A, zooming does make the two probe heights close in on 7.64 — the gap shrinks toward 0, which is exactly what it looks like for a limit to exist. But on Section C the gap sticks at 3 no matter how far you magnify: the left rail keeps arriving at 0 and the right rail keeps arriving at 3. Magnification does not create agreement, it only reveals whether the agreement was there. The classmate is confusing “I can’t see the gap” with “there is no gap,” and treating a limit as something zooming produces rather than something the function either has or doesn’t.
